## Before You Buy

### Financial Preparation
- **Deposit**: Typically 5-20% of purchase price
- **Savings**: Emergency fund for repairs and maintenance
- **Credit Score**: Good credit improves mortgage approval chances
- **Debt Review**: Minimize existing debt before applying

### Pre-Purchase Steps
1. Get pre-approved for a mortgage
2. Understand your budget and borrowing capacity
3. Research neighborhoods and property values
4. Get a property inspection
5. Review the LIM (Land Information Memorandum) report
6. Check title at LINZ

### Key Documents to Review
- **Title Deed**: Proof of ownership history
- **Certificate of Title**: Current legal ownership status
- **LIM Report**: Council information on zoning, building consent history
- **Building Inspection Report**: Condition assessment by qualified inspector
- **Survey**: Boundary and property dimensions

## The Purchase Process

### Step 1: Finding & Offer (1-2 weeks)
- View properties that meet your criteria
- Make an offer through a real estate agent
- Offer is typically conditional on finance, inspection, and LIM approval

### Step 2: Legal Review (1 week)
- Lawyer reviews contract and title
- Identify any issues or restrictions
- Negotiate conditions if needed

### Step 3: Due Diligence (2-4 weeks)
- **Finance**: Get formal mortgage approval
- **Inspection**: Hire building inspector and pest controller
- **Valuation**: Bank arranges property valuation
- **LIM Report**: Request from local council
- **Insurance Quotes**: Get home insurance quotes

### Step 4: Resolution of Conditions (2 weeks)
- Satisfy finance conditions
- Address any inspection issues
- Negotiate repairs or price adjustments

### Step 5: Settlement (1 week before)
- Final walkthrough of property
- Confirm settlement date and time
- Arrange moving logistics
- Notify utilities of move

### Step 6: Completion (Settlement Day)
- Lawyer transfers funds
- Seller receives payment
- Title transferred to buyer
- Keys exchanged
- You become the legal owner

## Costs of Home Ownership

### One-Time Purchase Costs
| Cost | Typical Amount | Notes |
|------|---|---|
| Deposit | 5-20% of price | Required before settlement |
| Legal Fees | $1,500-$3,000 | Lawyer fees for conveyancing |
| LIM Report | $300-$500 | Council information report |
| Building Inspection | $500-$1,500 | Professional building assessment |
| Valuation Fee | $400-$800 | Bank-required property valuation |
| Land Transfer Tax | 0-3.5% | Depends on property value & buyer |
| Title Registration | $200-$400 | LINZ registration costs |

### Ongoing Annual Costs
| Cost | Typical Amount | Notes |
|------|---|---|
| Rates (Council Tax) | $2,000-$5,000+ | Depends on location & property value |
| Home Insurance | $800-$2,000 | Contents & building insurance |
| Mortgage Interest | Variable | Depends on loan amount & rate |
| Maintenance | 1% of value | Plumbing, roofing, etc. |
| Utilities | $2,500-$4,000 | Power, water, gas, internet |

## Property Types

### Houses (Detached)
- **Advantages**: Land ownership, control over property
- **Disadvantages**: Higher maintenance, more expensive
- **Best For**: Families, larger properties, rural areas

### Townhouses & Terraced Homes
- **Advantages**: Lower price, shared maintenance costs
- **Disadvantages**: Less privacy, shared walls
- **Best For**: First-time buyers, urban areas

### Apartments (Flats)
- **Advantages**: Affordable, lower maintenance, security features
- **Disadvantages**: Smaller space, body corporate fees, leasehold issues
- **Best For**: First-time buyers, investors, urban professionals

### Sectional Title Property
- **Advantages**: Own your section, shared common areas
- **Disadvantages**: Body corporate levies, shared decision-making
- **Best For**: Urban areas, retirement living

## Financing Options

### Mortgage Types

#### Fixed Rate Mortgage
- **Rate**: Fixed for 6 months to 5 years
- **Advantages**: Predictable payments, protects from rate rises
- **Disadvantages**: May have higher rate, early exit fees

#### Floating Rate Mortgage
- **Rate**: Moves with market rates
- **Advantages**: Lower initial rate, no exit fees
- **Disadvantages**: Unpredictable payments, rises with rates

#### Split Mortgage
- **Structure**: Portion fixed, portion floating
- **Advantages**: Balance between stability and flexibility
- **Best For**: Uncertain market conditions

### Lenders

## Rights & Responsibilities After Purchase

### Your Rights
- Exclusive possession and use of property
- Right to lease or sell property
- Benefit from improvements and appreciation
- Legal protection against trespass

### Your Responsibilities
- Pay rates and property taxes
- Maintain property in good condition
- Comply with building codes and regulations
- Maintain adequate insurance
- Pay utilities and services
- Respect neighbors' rights
- Comply with council regulations

## Common Homeowner Issues

### Maintenance & Repairs
- Budget 1-2% of property value annually
- Keep records of all work completed
- Use qualified tradespersons
- Get insurance quotes for damage

### Rates & Taxes
- Pay council rates on time
- Understand what rates cover in your area
- Challenge valuations if appropriate
- Budget for rate increases

### Insurance
- Building insurance is essential
- Contents insurance protects belongings
- Income protection if mortgage dependent
- Life insurance if others depend on income

### Disputes
- Try negotiation first
- Use mediation services
- Consult a lawyer if needed
- Understand your dispute resolution options
